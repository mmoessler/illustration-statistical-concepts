Autoregressive moving-average (ARMA) Simulation

A Simulation study of ARMA processes

Topic of the module

ARMA process

A general ARMA(\(p,q\)) process is given by

$$ \begin{align} y_t = \delta + \alpha_1 y_{t-1} + ... + \alpha_p y_{t-p} + \epsilon_t - \beta_1 \epsilon_{t-1} - ... - \beta_q \epsilon_{t-q} \end{align} $$

with the alternative representation

$$ \begin{align} \alpha(L)y_t = \delta + \beta(L)\epsilon_t \end{align} $$

The stability condition is equivalent to that of an AR(\(p\)) process, i.e. the roots of the lag polynomial \(\alpha(L)= 0\)

must be greater than one in absolute value. Then an MA(\(\infty\)) or World representation of the process can be derived.


If the roots of \(\beta(L) = 0\) are also larger than one in absolute value, the process can be inverted to be represented

as a stationary infinite AR process.


From these properties it is evident, that neither the ACF nor the PACF of an ARMA(\(p,q\)) process breaks off.

Unit Circle with inverse roots of the polynomial

The figure shows the unit circle. When the inverse roots of the polynomial

are inside the unit circle, the process is stable. If the roots are on the outside or

directly on the circle, the process is unstable.

ACF Plot of the ARMA(p,q) process

The figure shows the ACF of the ARMA process.

  • For an AR(\(p\)) process, the ACF is geometrically declining.
  • For a MA(\(q\)) process, the ACF is non-zero up to lag \(q\), which gives the MA-order.
  • For an ARMA(\(p,q\)) process, the ACF is geometrically declining.
PACF Plot of the ARMA(p,q) process

The figure shows the PACF of the ARMA process.

  • For an AR(\(p\)) process, the PACF is non-zero up to lag \(p\), which gives the AR-order.
  • For a MA(\(q\)) process, the PACF is geometrically declining.
  • For an ARMA(\(p,q\)) process, the PACF is geometrically declining.
